Wing Chun Kung Fu

A Short Essay About Wing Chun...with More Content!

Wing Chun is a close range striking art developed in the late 17th century in Southern China that employs the philosophy of a centerline as a mode of attack and emphasizes “sticking“ to your opponent to control and dismantle structure. This is done through countless hours of sensitivity training, conditioning and building proper structure. It emphasizes bone structure and physics over strength and thus has been touted as a perfect system for people of smaller stature.

I started training Wing Chun in the summer of 2010. I had been looking for a good teacher for a long time and by the time I found someone, I was told he had moved back to San Diego. Luckily he still had a student in Sarasota by the name of Gabe Hernandez that had talked of starting up his own class. I began training under Gabe, and he would soon become one of my good friends. Then a year or so later his teacher, Rene, moved back into town, and I have been learning from him ever since. Rene is a second generation Yip Man disciple. For those that are not familiar with Yip Man (shown above), he was Bruce Lee’s first teacher and is considered to be the gold standard of Wing Chun practitioners. Many lineages will trace themselves back to him. There have been countless films in recent years made about him highlighting his life and the surrounding prestige. Most of it is exaggerated and overly dramatic, but you get the idea. His top disciple was a man named Ho Kam Ming, and one of Ho Kam Ming’s top students is Rene Ng, my teacher.
