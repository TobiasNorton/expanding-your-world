According to Yip Man, the story of Wing Chun was developed by a Buddhist nun by the name of Ng Mui in the 17th century after the destruction of the Henan Shaolin Monastery. She fled to the White Crane temple where she met a young girl by the name of Yim Wing-Chun who was being forced into marriage by a local bandit. Ng Mui taught Yim Wing Chun a style she had developed for practitioners to learn quickly and without much strength. This style was a stripped down version of the Snake and Crane styles and focused on economy of motion. She named the style after Yim Wing-Chun.
